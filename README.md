# Teen Mental Health & Social Media Analysis

## Overview
Analysis of mental health indicators for 1,200 teenagers aged 13–19, 
exploring the relationship between social media usage, sleep patterns, 
physical activity, academic performance, and depression. 
Built using Power BI.

## Key Findings
- Only 2.6% of teens (31 out of 1,200) show signs of depression
- Depressed teens average 4.8 hours of sleep vs 6.5 hours 
  for healthy peers — 35% less sleep
- Depressed teens show 8.5 average stress level vs 5.4 
  for healthy peers — 57% higher stress
- Social media hours do not predict depression — teens with 
  1 hour and 8 hours of daily usage show similar rates
- Platform choice has no significant impact — TikTok, Instagram, 
  and mixed users show nearly identical mental health indicators
- Physical activity shows surprisingly little stress reduction 
  across all activity groups
- Academic performance is not linked to screen time

## Tools Used
Power BI, Excel, DAX measures, calculated columns

## Data Source
Teen Mental Health Dataset — 1,200 records, ages 13–19
